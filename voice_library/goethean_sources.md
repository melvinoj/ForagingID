## A2. Published literature — uploaded PDFs (01 June 2026)

The strongest citable sources in the corpus. These supersede the scattered notes for anything going into the app, workshop materials, or future research.

| Source | What it contributes | Use in app |
|---|---|---|
| **Terry Irwin — *Audit / Comparison of Goethean Process* (Carnegie Mellon, drawn from Holdrege, Colquhoun, Brook, Hoffmann, Wahl, Seamon)** | Five practitioners' four-stage schemas laid out side by side, plus the Jungian-mandala mapping (Sensation/Feeling/Thinking/Intuition). Confirms the stages are robust across practitioners but that **Goethe himself set no fixed steps** — "any attempt to templatize his process too completely contradicts his primary objective." Crucial caution against over-rigid UI. | The canonical reference for the staged prompt sequence. Stage names + elemental qualities + the "Stage 0 / First Impression" pre-stage |
| **Isis Brook — *Engaging in the Goethean Method: An Approach for Understanding the Farm?* (Crossfields Institute; in *Subtle Agroecologies*, DOI 10.1201/9780429440939-23)** | The most **practical, step-by-step** account in the corpus. Choosing-the-thing ("waiting for it to call you"), the pre-stage of clearing habitual responses, drawing-from-memory ("should never be left out"), the discipline of staying in the present, **secondary sources held back until after first contact then treated sceptically — always return to the plant**, "imagining it otherwise" as a feel-for-wrongness test, Bockemühl's elemental framing, the summary table | Direct source for in-field prompt wording, the photo-as-anchor principle, and the "name/score pushed to background" rule. The secondary-source discipline maps almost exactly onto ForagingID's read-only-card / encounter-first architecture |
| **Terry Irwin & Seaton Baxter — *The Dynamical View of Natural Form* (Centre for the Study of Natural Design, Univ. Dundee; WIT Trans. on Ecology & the Environment)** | The **design-theory spine**: shift from *objects to relationships*; form as process in time; Goethe's **"absent whole"** (whole immanent in the parts, not their sum — Bortoft); the leaf-succession example; **Bortoft's flock-of-birds** (seeing *flying*, not separate birds); holistic vs analytic consciousness; place-based design | Grounds the entire "individual plant over time" thesis and the objects→relationships reframing of the encounters/species question. The "absent whole" is the precise philosophical name for what the archive builds |

## B. Key cited authors (from the dissertation) — for deeper grounding

- **Henri Bortoft** — *Taking Appearance Seriously* (Floris, 2012); *The Wholeness of Nature* (1996). The whole-in-the-part; critique of the Cartesian split; "no one way of revealing is exclusively true."
- **Craig Holdrege / The Nature Institute** — *Doing Goethean Science* (2005). Many thin dated returns cumulatively revealing the whole — the empirical argument for the encounter-over-time model.
- **Rudolf Steiner** — Goethean epistemology as praxis; *Anschauung*; the four stages' lineage.
- **Daniel Wahl** — "delicate empiricism" framing (2005).
- **Jean Gebser** — *The Ever-Present Origin*; a-perspectival awareness; dialectic nature of language.
- **Isis Brook** — on the imaginal stage as dreamlike; "any fixing puts us back into the first stage" (the voice-first rationale).
